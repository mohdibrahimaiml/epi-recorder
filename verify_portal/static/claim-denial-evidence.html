Insurance

When your AI denies a claim, what evidence do you have?

Insurance carriers are deploying AI for claim adjudication. When a claim is denied by an algorithm, regulators, policyholders, and courts will ask: what exactly did the AI do? EPI provides the answer — a cryptographically sealed, independently verifiable Decision Record.

The Problem

> CLAIM_ID: CH-2026-8842
> POLICY: Auto-Comprehensive
> AMOUNT: $4,200 (body damage)
> AI DECISION: DENIED

> REASON: "Pre-existing damage pattern"
> CONFIDENCE: 0.94

The policyholder appeals. The regulator investigates. The claim supervisor asks: "Show me exactly what the model saw, what rules it applied, and why it came to this conclusion." Without EPI, the answer is a scramble through cloud logs, model version history, and engineer memory.

With EPI

> EPI: claim-CH-2026-8842.epi
> SIGNED: Ed25519 | prod-signer
> VERIFIED: ✓ SHA-256 intact

> CONTAINS:
> • 23 execution steps (chain intact)
> • Model: claim-eval-v3.2 @ gpt-4
> • Policy rules applied
> • Images analyzed with metadata
> • Full reasoning trace

One file. Complete evidence. The supervisor opens it in any browser, sees the full decision trail — every input, every model call, every rule evaluation, every override. The file is cryptographically signed. It hasn't been modified. Case closed.
