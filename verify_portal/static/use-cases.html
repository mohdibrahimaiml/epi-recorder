Where EPI applies

Decisions that demand proof

EPI is built for any domain where an AI decision carries weight — financial, medical, legal, regulatory. When the question comes, the answer is a sealed file, not a scramble through logs.

💰

Financial Services

AI Loan · Fraud · Credit Scoring

When an AI model approves a mortgage, flags a transaction for AML review, or scores a credit application, regulators need to see exactly what happened. Not a summary. Not a dashboard. The full execution — every input, every model call, every intermediate decision — cryptographically sealed.

EPI captures the complete decision trail: model endpoint, prompt, response, tool invocations, and business rules applied. The resulting .epi file can be verified years later — long after the original model has been deprecated, the API keys rotated, and the infrastructure retired.

SR 11-7FINRAECB GuideFCRA
🏥

Healthcare & Medical AI

FDA · Clinical · Diagnostics

The FDA now reviews AI/ML-enabled medical devices through Predetermined Change Control Plans. Manufacturers must prove a model ran exactly as described in their 510(k) or De Novo submission — same version, same inputs, same outputs.

EPI produces a signed, immutable snapshot of every execution — the model fingerprint, the inference inputs, the structured output, the environment. A single file that any notified body or FDA reviewer can verify offline, without access to the manufacturer's cloud.

FDA 21 CFR 11PCCPSaMDMDR 2017/745
📋

Insurance Underwriting

Pricing · Claims · Governance

Colorado (SB 21-169), New York (DFS Circular No. 7), and the NAIC Model Bulletin now require insurers to demonstrate meaningful governance over AI used in pricing, underwriting, and claims adjudication. "The algorithm said so" is not enough.

EPI provides a portable, cryptographically verifiable audit trail — one file per decision. Every rating factor applied, every exclusion checked, every threshold evaluated, all sealed at decision time. The evidence survives the insurer's own infrastructure, making it independently auditable by regulators and third parties.

CO SB 21-169NY DFSNAICEIOPA

Government & Public Sector

Procurement · Benefits · Logs

The EU AI Act mandates that high-risk AI providers retain logs of operation (Art. 12) for a minimum of 10 years (Art. 19). Government agencies procuring AI systems need vendor-neutral evidence that outlives the vendor contract — evidence verifiable without the original supplier's software or infrastructure.

EPI is open-source, format-stable, and designed for decades-long retention. The .epi file is the artifact — self-contained, self-describing, and independently verifiable. A procurement officer in 2036 can verify a decision made in 2026 without installing proprietary software or tracking down a defunct vendor.

EU AI Act 12/19ProcurementFOIA

AI Safety & Evaluation

Red-Teaming · Benchmarks · Audits

Research labs, evaluation teams, and third-party auditors run thousands of controlled experiments on frontier models — probing for bias, toxicity, jailbreaking, and capability overhang. The results of these evaluations carry enormous weight, shaping deployment decisions and regulatory responses.

EPI seals every evaluation run: the exact prompt, the model version, the full multi-turn trajectory, the tool calls, and the final output. An independent reviewer can verify the full execution without access to the lab environment or model endpoint — essential for reproducible, defensible AI safety science.

Legal Technology

E-Discovery · Review · Custody

AI is increasingly embedded in document review, contract analysis, privilege classification, and e-discovery workflows. When the output of an AI tool enters a legal record, the chain of custody must be unbroken. A screenshot of a dashboard is not evidence.

EPI proves who ran the model, when, on which documents, with which parameters, and that nothing was modified after the fact. The .epi file serves as a verifiable case exhibit — admissible, auditable, and independent of the original tooling. One file that courts can trust.

FRCP 26(g)Chain of CustodyE-Discovery
Why EPI, not just logs

Observability tools — LangSmith, Weights & Biases, MLflow, Datadog — are built for development, not for portable, verifiable evidence. They live in your cloud, depend on your API keys, and vanish when you switch vendors.

EPI produces a single file containing the execution record, cryptographic proof, policy, fault analysis, and an embedded viewer. It verifies without the original infrastructure, without the original API keys, and without the original model. That is the difference between "we have logs somewhere" and "here is a sealed, independently verifiable case file."

What EPI does not do

EPI is not a compliance certification. It does not guarantee that an AI system is safe, fair, or regulatory-compliant. It does not detect hallucinations, evaluate model quality, or replace human oversight. EPI provides cryptographically verifiable evidence of execution. The auditor — the regulator, the court, the safety researcher — determines whether that evidence meets the threshold.
