WHERE EPI APPLIES

USE CASES

EPI does not dictate how you use AI. It provides verifiable proof of how AI was used — so when the question comes, the answer is a sealed file, not a scramble through logs.

FINANCIAL SERVICES

When an AI model decides a loan, flags a transaction, or scores credit, regulators need evidence. EPI captures the full decision trail — model input, reasoning, tool calls, output — and seals it with an Ed25519 signature that can be verified years later, even if the original infrastructure no longer exists.

HEALTHCARE & MEDICAL AI

The FDA now reviews AI/ML devices through Predetermined Change Control Plans (PCCPs). Manufacturers must prove a model ran exactly as described in their 510(k). EPI produces a signed, immutable snapshot of every execution — in a single file that any regulator can verify offline.

INSURANCE UNDERWRITING

Colorado (SB 21-169), New York (DFS Circular), and California now require insurers to demonstrate governance over AI used in pricing, underwriting, and claims. EPI provides a portable audit trail — one file per decision, independently verifiable — that maps directly to NAIC principles and state-level explainability rules.

GOVERNMENT & PUBLIC SECTOR

The EU AI Act requires high-risk AI providers to retain logs for 10 years. Government agencies procuring AI systems need vendor-neutral evidence that survives infrastructure turnover. EPI is open-source, format-stable, and verifiable without the original vendor's software. The evidence package IS the artifact.

AI SAFETY & EVALUATION

Research labs, red-teaming exercises, and safety evaluations generate sensitive data about model behavior. EPI captures every step with a cryptographically linked chain. Independent reviewers can verify the full execution without access to the original lab environment — essential for reproducible AI safety science.

LEGAL TECHNOLOGY

AI is increasingly used for document review, contract analysis, and e-discovery. When the output of an AI tool becomes part of a legal record, the chain of custody matters. EPI proves who ran the model, when, what it produced, and that nothing was modified — a verifiable case file, not a screenshot.

WHY EPI, NOT JUST LOGS

Debugging tools (LangSmith, Weights & Biases, MLflow, Datadog) are built for development — not for portable, verifiable evidence. EPI produces one file containing the execution record, policy, cryptographic proof, fault analysis, and an embedded viewer. It verifies without the original infrastructure, the original API keys, or the original model. The difference between "we have logs somewhere" and "here is a sealed, independently verifiable case file."

WHAT EPI DOES NOT DO

EPI is not a compliance certification. It does not guarantee that an AI system is safe, fair, or regulatory-compliant. It provides cryptographically verifiable evidence of execution — the auditor determines whether that evidence satisfies a given regulatory threshold. EPI also does not detect hallucinations, evaluate model quality, or replace human oversight.
